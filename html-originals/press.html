Key facts for reporters
Rhode Island first
Launching in Rhode Island in Summer 2026, with Massachusetts expansion planned within 6 months of launch. Building local before going national.
Flat rate vs. pay-per-lead
Competitors like Thumbtack and HomeAdvisor charge $25–$80 per lead. Property Pros charges $9.99/month flat — unlimited leads, no per-quote fees.
Two-tier verification
Licensed trades (roofing, electrical, plumbing, HVAC) require state license and insurance certificate. Other trades are verified by phone and email.
Three user types
Built for homeowners (free), service contractors ($9.99/mo), and real estate agents ($29.99/mo) who need to coordinate repair work for clients.
Two-way rating system
Homeowners rate contractors, and contractors rate homeowners. This creates accountability on both sides and helps pros identify serious clients.
Plus™ gives back
Homeowners who subscribe to Plus™ ($9.99/year) can direct 50% of their fee to a local nonprofit including Habitat for Humanity RI and the RI Community Food Bank.
About Property Pros

Property Pros is a Rhode Island-based home services marketplace built to fix a problem that affects every homeowner: finding a reliable, fairly priced local contractor is too hard, and the existing platforms put the cost of that difficulty on the contractor in the form of per-lead fees.

Founded in 2026, Property Pros charges service professionals a flat monthly subscription — $9.99/month — rather than charging per lead. This means contractors can quote every project that fits their trade without financial risk, and homeowners receive more and better quotes as a result.

The platform serves three audiences: homeowners who need to find and hire contractors, service professionals who want a sustainable way to grow their business without paying per-lead fees, and real estate agents who regularly coordinate repair work for buyers and sellers navigating transactions.

Property Pros is launching in Rhode Island in Summer 2026 with a focus on building a high-quality local marketplace before expanding to Massachusetts and beyond.
