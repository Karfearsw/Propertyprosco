Dispute Resolution

We're here to help if something goes wrong

Most issues resolve through direct communication. When they don't, Property Pros steps in to help mediate fairly.

The resolution process
We follow a clear 5-step process to resolve disputes between homeowners and contractors as fairly and quickly as possible.
1
Step 1
Contact the other party directly
Most disputes resolve when both parties communicate clearly. Use the Property Pros messaging system to explain the issue in writing. This creates a record of the conversation if escalation is needed.
Try this first — give 48 hours for a response
2
Step 2
Open a dispute with Property Pros
If direct communication doesn't resolve the issue, contact our support team at support@propertypros.com with your project ID. Include photos, messages, and any documentation. We'll open a formal case within 1 business day.
Response within 1 business day
3
Step 3
We review the evidence
Our team reviews all on-platform messages, the original project scope, submitted quotes, photos, and any additional documentation provided by both parties. We may ask follow-up questions to clarify the situation.
Typically 3–5 business days
4
Step 4
We issue a recommendation
Based on our review, we issue a written recommendation for resolution. This might include a partial refund, requiring the contractor to return and fix the work, or in clear cases of misconduct, removing the contractor from the platform.
Written decision within 7 business days total
5
Step 5
External escalation if needed
Property Pros is a marketplace — we are not a party to the contract between you and the contractor. If our mediation doesn't resolve the issue and significant money is at stake, we can provide your documentation to support small claims court or a licensing board complaint.
See escalation options below

Protect yourself before the job starts
These four habits prevent the vast majority of disputes.
Get the scope in writing
Before any work starts, make sure the quote or message describes exactly what's included — materials, labor, cleanup, and what's explicitly excluded.
Keep all communication on-platform
Messages sent through Property Pros are recorded and can be reviewed during a dispute. Off-platform agreements are very difficult to enforce.
Take before and after photos
Document the condition of the property before work begins and after it's done. Photos are the most valuable evidence in any dispute.
Hire verified contractors
Licensed & Insured contractors have uploaded their state license and insurance certificate. If something goes wrong, there's a real business and policy behind the work.
External escalation options
If our mediation doesn't resolve the issue, these are the appropriate authorities for different situations in Rhode Island.
Small Claims Court
For disputes up to $2,500 in Rhode Island. No lawyer required. File at your local District Court. Property Pros can provide your message history and documentation.
courts.ri.gov →
RI Contractor Licensing Board
For complaints against licensed contractors in RI. The board can investigate and revoke licenses. File a complaint if a licensed contractor did defective work or abandoned a project.
crb.ri.gov →
RI Attorney General
For consumer protection complaints. The AG's office handles contractor fraud, deceptive practices, and cases where a contractor took payment and didn't perform work.
riag.ri.gov →
Local Police
If a contractor took a significant payment (deposit) and abandoned the project without doing work, this may constitute fraud or theft. Contact your local police department.
Emergency: 911 · Non-emergency: 311
Common questions
How do I open a dispute?
Email support@propertypros.com with your project ID and a description of the issue. Include any photos and the relevant messages from the platform. We'll open a case within 1 business day.
What if the contractor left a bad review in retaliation?
We take retaliatory reviews seriously. If you believe a review was left in retaliation for a legitimate complaint, contact support with the dispute case ID and we'll investigate and may remove the review.
The contractor wants payment before the job is complete. Is that normal?
A deposit (typically 10–30%) is normal for larger jobs to cover materials. Avoid paying more than 50% upfront for any job. Never pay in full before work is completed. If a contractor is demanding full payment upfront, that's a red flag.
A contractor on the platform isn't licensed. What should I do?
Report it immediately to support@propertypros.com. We will verify their credentials and remove them from the platform if they are misrepresenting their license status. Only hire Licensed & Insured badge contractors for trades that require a state license in RI.
Does Property Pros offer any kind of work guarantee?
We are a marketplace connecting homeowners with independent contractors. We do not guarantee the quality of work or act as the contractor. However, we vet all contractors, verify licenses and insurance for applicable trades, and take disputes seriously — including removing contractors who receive legitimate complaints.

Need to open a dispute?

Email us with your project ID and we'll respond within 1 business day.
