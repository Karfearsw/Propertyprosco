Section 1
Setting up your profile
Your profile is your storefront. Homeowners review it before accepting a quote. A complete, professional profile gets significantly more responses than one with missing information.
Profile checklist
✓
Business name and professional photo or logo
✓
Bio: describe your experience, specialties, and what makes you different (3–5 sentences minimum)
✓
Services offered: be specific — list every trade you do
✓
Years in business and any certifications
✓
At least 5 portfolio photos (see Section 3)
✓
Response time commitment — set expectations
💡 Profiles with a bio over 3 sentences receive 2x more quote views than bare-minimum profiles.
Section 2
Verification tiers
Property Pros has two verification tiers. The tier you're in depends on your trade. Both tiers show a badge on your profile — but the Licensed & Insured badge is more trusted by homeowners and is required for certain projects.
Licensed & Insured

Licensed & Insured

Section 3
Portfolio photos
Photos are the single most powerful thing on your profile. Homeowners want to see your actual work, not just read about it. Aim for at least 10 high-quality photos showing a variety of jobs.
What makes a great portfolio photo
✓
Before and after pairs — these are the most compelling
✓
Natural lighting when possible — avoid flash glare
✓
Wide shot showing the full scope of work
✓
Close-up detail shots showing quality of craftsmanship
✓
Caption each photo with the trade, material, and city
✓
Horizontal (landscape) orientation photographs better on the platform
📸 Profiles with 5+ photos receive 3x more quote acceptances. Even photos from your phone are fine — quality matters more than equipment.
Section 4
Setting your service area
Your service area determines which projects you see. Set it accurately — being matched to a project 50 miles away that you won't do wastes everyone's time and hurts your response rate metric.
Service area tips
✓
Start with a realistic radius from your base — typically 15–30 miles
✓
You can set different radii for different services (e.g. bigger for roofing, smaller for handyman)
✓
Expand your area once you've built a solid review count
✓
Update your area seasonally if you have slow months in certain zones
Section 5
How leads work
When a homeowner posts a project that matches your trade and service area, it appears in your Leads dashboard. You'll get a notification. From there you can view the details, save the lead for later, or send a quote immediately.
Lead types
🔥
Hot leads — posted recently, high budget match, few quotes sent. Quote these first.
★
Matched leads — strong match to your trade and area. High probability projects.
📋
All leads — every project in your service area across all match levels.
⚡ Pros who respond to a lead within 2 hours are 3x more likely to win the job than those who respond the next day. Enable push notifications.
Section 6
Writing a winning quote
Your quote is your first impression. A generic one-line quote with a number rarely wins. A personalized, specific quote that references the homeowner's project shows you actually read the details.
Quote formula that works
1
Reference something specific from their project (e.g. "I saw you mentioned a 10-year-old asphalt driveway...")
2
Describe exactly what you'll do — include materials, process, and cleanup
3
Give your price with a clear breakdown — labor vs. materials if possible
4
State your availability and estimated timeline
5
Invite them to ask questions — keep the conversation going
💬 Quotes that end with a question or invitation to chat have a 60% higher response rate. Don't just send a number and wait.
Section 7
Building your rating
Reviews are your most valuable long-term asset on the platform. Pros with 10+ reviews consistently win more quotes than equally priced competitors with fewer reviews. Start asking from day one.
How to get more reviews
✓
Ask in person at job completion — "Would you mind leaving a quick review on Property Pros?"
✓
Send a follow-up message through the platform a day after the job
✓
Respond to every review — both positive and negative — professionally
✓
Fix issues that appear in reviews before they become a pattern
⭐ Your first 5 reviews matter most. Getting to a 4.5+ rating with 5+ reviews puts you in the top results for most search and filter combinations.
